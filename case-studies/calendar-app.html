Completing a Full Redesign to Boost Success Rates by 23%

Improving key UX metrics through a data-driven redesign.

Executive Summary

I completed a full redesign of this product over 3 months, delivering measurable user experience improvements: 31-second reduction in task completion time, 23% increase in success rate, single-attempt task completion, and satisfaction scores that jumped from 74% to 95%.

The redesign involved overhauling all existing features and designing 3 new features, creating intuitive workflows that eliminated friction points and dramatically improved both efficiency and user satisfaction.

Role:

UX Designer / Project Manager

Duration:

3 months

Responsibilites:

UX Research, UX Design, Wireframing, Prototyping, and Project Managment

Introduction

What is the UVU Schedule Building App?

The UVU scheduling app is a long-term project conducted by the digital media department. The app serves as a tool to decrease the time and effort that department admins spend on creating printed classroom schedules which are displayed on the doors of the rooms of classrooms and offices in our department.

Admins currently accomplish through a long an arduous process that takes them weeks to complete.

After Taking a Look, I had a Hunch...

I loaded up the current build of this product and found it to be a very simple product. However, I felt that some of the features of the current design were not very intuitive.

All actions for the product were contained in 6 buttons in the top right corner of the screen, I found some of the micro-copy to not be descriptive and it took me about 15 minutes to figure out how this product worked.

I felt these issues warranted a redesign, but I wanted to confirm this hunch with thorough user research before committing our team's resources to full redesign.

Arming Myself with Data

Getting Buy-in From Stakeholders

I wanted to make sure that everyone involved with the project was looped in, so I created a research plan to present to my team and decision-makers. Once I had approval I carried our research and analysis.

Research Overview

Research Questions

  • How effective is the current design?
  • Which features need to be improved?
  • What new features should be added?

Usability Metrics

  • Effectiveness: Success Rate
  • Efficiency: Time based efficiency & Task attempts
  • Satisfaction: Task level satisfaction scores

Testing Methods

I designed a study that mixed a usability test with interview questions.

  • 10 interview questions

Testing Methods

My testing revealed to me that there was significant room in performance.

Breaking the success rates and satisfaction scores by task, it is easy to see which features are in need of overhaul. There were 3 tasks which performed much worse than other tasks in the test.

Through analyzing the qualitative data collected during this, I identified 4 new necessary features to add to our roadmap.

The Redesign

Empty and Disabled States

My research found that users were overwhelmed and confused by being dumped straight into an empty calendar. I created an empty state to inform users of what they need to do to get started.

New Look & New Sidebar

The main options for manipulating have been moved to a new sidebar. This prevents these options from being buried and lowers the amount of clicks necessary in the workflow.
